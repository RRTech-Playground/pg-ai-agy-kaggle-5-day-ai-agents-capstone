# FORDEC & Crew Resource Management (CRM) Decision-Making Principles

In high-consequence environments like aviation, decision-making under stress is structured to counter cognitive biases, tunnel vision, and emotional escalation. This document details the FORDEC framework and associated CRM principles.

## The FORDEC Framework

FORDEC is a structured decision-making model designed to optimize cognitive load and team alignment during critical situations:

### 1. Facts (What is the actual situation?)
- **Objective vs. Subjective**: Distinguish verified data (e.g., flight instrument readings, fuel status) from assumptions or inferences.
- **Data Integrity**: Under pressure, humans tend to fill information gaps with assumptions. The first step requires listing only hard, verifiable facts.

### 2. Options (What courses of action are available?)
- **Divergent Thinking**: Generate multiple distinct pathways (e.g., continue flight, divert to alternate airport, hold pattern).
- **Avoid Binary Traps**: Under stress, decision-makers often fall into "either-or" traps. Forcing the listing of at least three options helps break this bias.

### 3. Risks (What are the hazards of each option?)
- **Mitigation Assessment**: For each option, evaluate the worst-case scenarios and the probability of occurrence.
- **Secondary Risks**: Analyze how choosing an option might introduce new, unforeseen risks.

### 4. Decision (Which option is chosen and why?)
- **Clear Selection**: Explicitly choose the path that maximizes safety and success probability based on the Option-Risk matrix.
- **Command Intent**: Clearly articulate the chosen option to all team members/agents.

### 5. Execution (How do we implement the decision?)
- **Action Plan**: Define step-by-step responsibilities and actions.
- **Resource Allocation**: Assign tasks to balance workload and prevent bottlenecking of any single actor.

### 6. Check (How do we monitor the outcome?)
- **Feedback Loops**: Define specific trigger points or times to re-evaluate the decision.
- **Dynamic Adaptability**: If the situation changes or the checks show unexpected outcomes, restart the FORDEC cycle immediately.

## Crew Resource Management (CRM) & Stress-Testing

CRM focuses on optimizing human performance, communication, and situational awareness:

### Cognitive Biases Targeted
1. **Plan Continuation Bias (Get-there-itis)**: The tendency to continue a plan of action even when conditions change and make it unsafe. FORDEC's **Check** and **Risks** phases directly combat this.
2. **Tunnel Vision (Target Fixation)**: Focus on a single instrument or problem while ignoring the broader state. The **Facts** phase forces a systemic scan of all parameters.
3. **Authority Gradients**: The breakdown of critical feedback due to hierarchical pressure. Structured protocols ensure all crew members can voice concerns during the **Options** and **Risks** phases.
